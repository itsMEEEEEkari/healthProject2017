{Obsessive Compulsive Disorder}

Obsessive Compulsive disorder is a disorder that causes people to experience obsessions and Compulsions. Obsessions are also known as intrusive thoughts, or unwanted thoughts that causes anxiety or fear. Compulsions are things a person does, like checking, cleaning, and rearranging to satisfy the anxiety caused by the obsessions. A person with this disorder may recognize that their anxieties are irrational, but trying to stop their compulsions often causes more anxiety. This disorder is fairly common and can be caused by a person’s biology, environment, or genetics.

Signs and Symptoms

Fear of contamination

Excessive worrying

Stress when things aren’t perfectly in order

Unwanted thoughts about harming yourself or others

Excessive washing and cleaning

Constantly checking

Over-orderliness

Very routined

Need reassurance

Statistics

1% of the United States adult population has OCD.

Help

Psychotherapy:

  • Cognitive Behovior Therapy
  • Exposure and Response Therapy

Medication:

  • Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRI)
  • Tricyclic Antidepressants
  • Some antipsychotics
